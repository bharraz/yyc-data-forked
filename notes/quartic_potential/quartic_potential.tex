\section{Goal}
Figure out the relation between some of the important parameters
for a double-well formed by quartic potential

\section{Parameters}
Focusing on the two wells, the intuitive parameters includes
\begin{enumerate}
\item Confinement for each well (the coefficient of $x^2$)
\item Well separation
\item Well height difference
\end{enumerate}
The barrier height between the wells could be useful as well.

\section{Setup}
Let's say one of the well (let's say the left one) is located at $(0, 0)$ and has the form $\dfrac{x^2}2$. The most generic quartic potential we can write is then
\eqar{
  U=&\frac{ax^4}{4}+\frac{bx^3}{3}+\frac{x^2}{2}
}
note that the trap centering at $(0, 0)$ eliminates the linear and constant terms.

\section{Derivation}
Derivatives
\eqar{
  U'=&ax^3+bx^2+x\\
  U''=&3ax^2+2bx+1
}
At the bottom of the well(s) (and the top of the barrier) we have
\eqar{
  0=&U'\\
  =&ax^3+bx^2+x\\
  =&(ax^2+bx+1)x
}
Solution $x=0$ is the one we fixed and for the other well and the barrier we have
\eqar{
  ax^2=&-(bx + 1)
}
Assuming a real solution $x$ exist
\eqar{
  U''=&3ax^2+2bx+1\\
  =&-3(bx + 1)+2bx+1\\
  =&-bx - 2\\
  U=&\frac{-(bx + 1)x^2}{4}+\frac{bx^3}{3}+\frac{x^2}{2}\\
  =&-\frac{-bx^3}{4}-\frac{x^2}{4}+\frac{bx^3}{3}+\frac{x^2}{2}\\
  =&\frac{bx^3}{12}+\frac{x^2}{4}\\
  =&\frac{x^2}{4}\paren{1 + \frac{bx}3}
}
We can pick the position of the second well $x_2$ and $\alpha\equiv -bx_2 - 2$
as the free parameters.

\begin{enumerate}
\item The confirment for the second well, relative to the first well is $\alpha$.
\item The well separation is simply $x_2$.
\item The well height difference is $\dfrac{x_2^2\paren{1 - \alpha}}{12}$
\end{enumerate}
Note that this can describe both the second well and the barrier.
The condition for this describing the well, instead of the barrier is simply $\alpha>0$.

With a fixed ratio of the potential the well height difference goes down quadratically and the only way to get true zero well height differences is for $\alpha=1$, which means symmetric well.
